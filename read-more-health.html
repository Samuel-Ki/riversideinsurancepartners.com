Health Insurance

Health insurance can help manage the cost of medical care, including routine appointments, emergency treatment, hospital services, and prescription medications. Available plans and benefits may vary by provider, location, and eligibility.

Coverage may include:
  • Primary and specialist doctor visits
  • Preventive care and wellness services
  • Emergency and urgent care
  • Hospital and surgical services
  • Prescription drug coverage
  • Laboratory testing and diagnostic imaging
  • Mental and behavioral health services

Medicare

Medicare provides federal health coverage for eligible individuals. Understanding the different parts of Medicare and the additional coverage options available can help you select coverage suited to your healthcare and prescription needs.

Medicare options may include:
  • Original Medicare Part A hospital coverage
  • Original Medicare Part B medical coverage
  • Medicare Advantage plans
  • Medicare Part D prescription drug plans
  • Medicare Supplement insurance
  • Dental, vision, and hearing benefits on certain plans
  • Plan reviews during eligible enrollment periods
Ask About Medicare

Long-Term Care Insurance

Long-term care insurance can help pay for certain services needed when a person requires ongoing assistance with daily activities because of illness, injury, disability, or age-related conditions.

Coverage may help with:
  • In-home care services
  • Assisted living facilities
  • Nursing home care
  • Adult day care services
  • Respite care for family caregivers
  • Personal care and daily living assistance
  • Care coordination services
